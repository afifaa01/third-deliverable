Morning Night Weekly
Hydrating Cleanser (optional).
Start your day by using a gentle cleaner to wipe away excess oil and dirt.		 Cleanser.
Wipe away makeup, dirt, debris with a clarifying cleanser or one of your choosing. 		Exfoliator
If you have concerns with texture, this would be a perfect time to use an exfoliator pad or an exfoliating toner. Be sure to not overuse it. Try it once or twice a week first.
Toner.
If you have oily skin, add a mattifying toner to control oil for the rest of your day.		 Toner
add hydration back into your face. 		Chemical Exfoliator
Chemical exfoliators will be AHAs, BHAs and PHAs. These will help clean out your pores, reduce blackheads, and give you smoother skin.
Moisturizer
To nourish your skin, add a lightweight moisturizer on your face to keep it hydrated.		 Treatment Serum
Treat your face with a serum for hyperpigmentation or dullness, or texture. 		Physical Exfoliator
These are basically face scrubs. Not suited for most people, but if you do not have sensitive skin.
SPF/Vitamin C
Arguably the most important step, add sunscreen to protect your face from sun damages.		 Moisturizer
add a night mask if you'd like as well, keep your skin nourished through the night.		 Mask
Treat yourself with a mask once in a while. Collagen masks are great for your skin, hyaluronic masks will add the extra hydration. If you want to treat clogged pores, choose a clay mask!
